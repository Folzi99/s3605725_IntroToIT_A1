Insights From Various Personality Tests:

What do the results of these tests mean for you?

The results of these tests are cautiously relatable. I attempted to answer the questions as truthfully as possible and I do believe, to some extent, that there is some truth to these questionnaires. However, my understanding is that the validity of these tests is highly debated amongst the broader scientific community. I think from a distance these results can offer insight into our own character but I also believe that reading a horoscope would serve a similar purpose. Although, I do believe that they can provide insight into new terminology to help individuals/business determine what life/working style choice might lead to better satisfaction. I also don’t believe it's fair to assume that personality is linear, as we experience certain things in life and grow older, our perceptions and personality would change. What I found interesting from the Myers Briggs test is that when I took it several years ago my personality type was in a different category. I think these results only offer a snapshot of who we are at a particular moment in time.

How do you think these results may influence your behaviour in a team?

I don't believe basing my decisions and interactions on these results alone, would significantly impact my behaviour in a team. If I were in a team, I think sharing our results, in a team building exercise, and acknowledging that these results are not the be-all-and-end-all of each individual can allow us to learn more about each other and develop a strong relationship.

How should you take this into account when forming a team?

Having an understanding of these various results can help determine what role I may be more suited for in a team. However, I don’t think the results should constrain me to one style of working if I feel I have the capability to transition to something else.
